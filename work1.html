"BODIES, BODIES, BODIES"

A Confessional by Nash Pena, MOODY MAGAZINE








I work as a graphic design intern for Moody Magazine, which is a syracuse based magazine that explores sexuality and mental health, and I was tasked with creating a graphic for "Bodies, Bodies, Bodies: A confessional" by Nash Pena. This passage explores Nash's struggle with a dark and metaphorical burden from his past. He has a metaphorical closet filled with "bodies" that represent unresolved issues, secrets, or traumatic experiences. Initially, the narrator tried to hide and ignore these problems, but over time, they accumulated and became an ever-present source of distress. The arrival of a new person in Nash's life, "him," triggers a change. Nash begins to share their life with this person, and their relationship becomes meaningful. However, Nash is still haunted by the "bodies" of his past, represented by disturbing sounds, smells, and sensations. These unresolved issues threaten his newfound happiness. After reading this, I felt compelled to add and represent pieces of his story into a preview of sorts.
